个人介绍

谭敏佳，中国科学院上海药物研究所研究员、博士生导师、课题组长。2003 年毕业于复旦大学，2008 年获得中科院上海药物所博士学位，并于2008 年至2012年在美国芝加哥大学从事博士后研究。实验室围绕基于蛋白组学技术的蛋白修饰调控和药物精准干预开展研究。发表SCI论文100余篇，其中近五年以通讯作者（含共同）发表在Cell（2篇）、Cell Metab、Mol Cell、Nat Commun等知名国际学术期刊。

Dr. Minjia Tan obtained Ph.D. degree in Shanghai Institute of Shanghai Institute of Materia Medica, Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS), and got post-doctoral training in Dr. Yingming Zhao’s lab at Ben May Department of Cancer Research, the University of Chicago. He is currently a professor in Chemical Proteomics Center, Shanghai Institute of Materia Medica, Chinese Academy of Sciences. Dr. Tan’s interest is focused on the development of new mass spectrometry-based proteomics technologies and utilizes these technologies to characterize the roles of protein post-translational modifications (PTMs) in physiology and diseases, and explore new therapeutic approaches.

讲解摘要

Protein post-translational modifications (PTMs) play fundamental roles in cellular physiology and disease development. Yet current understanding of the inventory and function of PTMs is by far limited. In this talk, I will present our recent study on the systematic characterization of PTMs in several types of disease models and clinical lung cancer samples using mass spectrometry-based proteomics technologies. These studies led to the identification of new mechanisms, biomarkers, and therapeutic approaches in diseases.

Micro-flow liquid chromatography tandem mass spectrometry (μLC–MS/MS) is becoming a viable alternative to nano-flow LC–MS/MS for the analysis of proteomes. We demonstrated the potential of such a system operating with a 1 mm i.d. × 150 mm column and at a flow rate of 50 μL/min for high-throughput, robust and reproducible proteomics on the basis of the analysis of ∼38,000 samples over the past two years. Up to 15,00 analyses were performed within one month, and >14,000 samples could be analyzed on a single column without loss of chromatographic performance. Different types of samples and acquisition methods were tested, more than 7,000 proteins could be quantified by either single shot DDA or DIA analysis with 2 hour gradient, demonstrating its great potential in proteomics research.
