讲解摘要

Fully understanding the target spaces of drugs is essential for investigating the mechanism of drug action and side effect, as well as for drug discovery and repositioning. In this study, we present an energetics-based approach, termed as pH-dependent protein precipitation (pHDPP), to probe the ligand-induced protein stability shift for proteome-wide drug target identification. We demonstrate that pHDPP works for a diverse array of ligands, yielding highly specific identification of target proteins from total cell lysate. This approach is compared to thermal and solvent-induced denaturation approaches with a pan-kinase inhibitor as the model drug, demonstrating its high sensitivity and high complementarity to other approaches. pHDPP was applied to reveal the target space of Dihydroartemisinin (DHA) and 45 target proteins were identified. Pathways analysis indicated these target proteins are mainly involved in metabolism and apoptosis pathways. Two cancer-related target proteins, ALDH7A1 and HMGB1, were validated by the structural simulation and AI-based target prediction methods. And they were further validated to have strong affinity with DHA by using cellular thermal shift assay (CETSA). In summary, pHDPP is a powerful tool to construct target protein space to reveal the mechanism of drug action and would have broad application in drug discovery studies.
