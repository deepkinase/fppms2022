个人介绍

李顺成博士自2019年联合创始嘉华药锐以来担任公司首席科学官。他还是加拿大西安大略大学生物化学系终身教授，加拿大功能基因组学、蛋白质组学和癌症分子机制研究主席。李博士在《自然》、《细胞》、《科学》或它们的姊妹期刊等高影响力科学期刊上发表了 150 多篇文章。他是国际公认的蛋白质组学和磷酸化蛋白质组学专家。李博士获得了无数奖项和认可，包括沃尔夫教授职位（2018 年）、加拿大邮务新枫采创业荣誉大奖（2016 年）、院长卓越奖（2016 年、2015 年、2004 年）、勃林格殷格翰青年研究员奖（2004 年）、加拿大国家癌症研究所颁发的卓越研究奖（2001 年）和 Harold E Johns 奖（2001 年）。李博士在多伦多大学获得博士学位（生物化学），在上海生物化学与细胞生物学研究所获得硕士学位，在北京大学获得学士学位。他曾在世界知名分子生物学家 Tony Pawson 实验室接受博士后训练，Tony Pawson对 SH2 结构域的发现改变了信号转导、癌症研究和药物发现领域

Dr. Shawn Shun-Cheng Li has served as Chief Scientific Officer of DeepKinase since he co-founded the company in 2019. He is also tenured professor at the Department of Biochemistry, Western University, Canada and Canada Research Chair in the Functional Genomics, Proteomics, and Molecular Mechanism of Cancer. Dr. Li has published more than 150 articles in high-impact scientific journals including Nature, Cell, Science or their sister journals. He is an internationally recognized expert in proteomics and phospho-proteomics. Dr. Li is recipient of numerous awards and recognitions, including Wolfe Professorship (2018), Canada Post Mandarin Profile Grand Award (2016), Dean’s Award of Excellence (2016, 2015, 2004), Boehringer Ingelheim Young Investigator Award (2004), Premier’s Research Excellence Award (2001) and Harold E Johns Award from the National Cancer Institute of Canada (2001). Dr. Li received his PhD degree (Biochemistry) from the University of Toronto, MSc from the Shanghai Institute of Biochemistry and Cell Biology, and BSc from Peking University. He was trained as a post-doctoral fellow with Tony Pawson, a world-renowned molecular biologist whose discovery of the SH2 domain has transformed the field of signal transduction, cancer research and drug discovery.

讲解摘要(暂无）

作为纳升色谱串联质谱技术的一种替代技术，微升液相色谱串联质谱系统逐渐被应用于蛋白质组学研究。在过去2年多的时间内，通过38000个蛋白质组学样品的分析，我们证明了使用1 mm内径色谱柱和50 μL/min流速的微升液相色谱串联质谱系统具有极高的分析通量、稳定性和重现性。该系统可以每个月分析1500个样品，在色谱分离效率没有明显损失的情况下，单根色谱柱分析了超过14000个样品。我们测试了不同的样品类型和不同的数据采集方法，2小时的单针进样DDA和DIA分析都可以定量到7000种以上的蛋白质，证明了该微升液相色谱串联质谱系统在蛋白质组学研究领域具有极高的应用前景。


Micro-flow liquid chromatography tandem mass spectrometry (μLC–MS/MS) is becoming a viable alternative to nano-flow LC–MS/MS for the analysis of proteomes. We demonstrated the potential of such a system operating with a 1 mm i.d. × 150 mm column and at a flow rate of 50 μL/min for high-throughput, robust and reproducible proteomics on the basis of the analysis of ∼38,000 samples over the past two years. Up to 15,00 analyses were performed within one month, and >14,000 samples could be analyzed on a single column without loss of chromatographic performance. Different types of samples and acquisition methods were tested, more than 7,000 proteins could be quantified by either single shot DDA or DIA analysis with 2 hour gradient, demonstrating its great potential in proteomics research.
